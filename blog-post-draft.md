# Kiln Loading 101: Maximize Space Without Disasters

Loading a kiln can feel like a high-stakes game of Tetris. You want to fit as much as possible to be efficient with your firing energy, but one wrong move—a piece too close to a heating element, a glaze drip, or a wobbly stack—can ruin an entire kiln load of hard work. 

Here are my top tips for loading like a pro:

1. **Space is your friend**: Keep at least an inch between your pots and the kiln elements. It ensures even airflow and prevents damage.
2. **Glaze control**: Leave at least 1/4 inch of unglazed foot on every piece. If you're experimenting with runny glazes, use cookies!
3. **Sturdy bases**: Ensure your shelf posts are perfectly level. A wobble in the kiln usually leads to a crack (or a crash) when you're firing.
4. **Think in layers**: Place your tallest, heaviest items on the bottom and work your way up to smaller, lighter pieces.
5. **Listen to your kiln**: If a shelf sounds hollow or has hairline cracks, retire it. Better to lose a shelf than your favorite series of mugs.

Happy firing!
