## PROMO #2: General — Why I Created This App

**Caption/Post:**

I've been working with clay since I was a kid.

And after all these years, the #1 problem hasn't changed: **"What clay and glaze did I use on THAT piece?"**

Every potter I know has been there. You pull something out of the kiln and it's gorgeous — and you have NO idea what you did. You scribbled it on a napkin, or maybe a notebook that's buried under a pile of clay scraps somewhere. Or maybe you just... didn't write it down at all. 😩

That's why I built **The Potter's Mud Room**.

It's a free app — built by a real potter, for real potters — that lets you:

🏺 **Track every piece** from wet clay to finished work
🎨 **Log your glazes** — commercial AND recipes, with photos of results
🪨 **Record clay bodies** — shrinkage %, cost per bag, wet vs fired color
🔥 **Keep firing logs** — cone, atmosphere, speed, holds, results
💰 **Track your sales** — by venue, with CSV export for tax season
💬 **Join the community** — a forum where potters help potters, no gatekeeping

This isn't some generic app made by a tech company that's never touched clay. This was built because I NEEDED it. And I know you need it too.

The free tier gives you 20 pieces, a personal clay and glaze library, and forum access — no credit card required. When you're ready for more, paid plans start at just **$4.99/mo** with our Founding Member rate.

We work on every device — phone, tablet, desktop. Use it at the studio, at shows, at your kitchen table. Wherever you make your work.

I built this because I believe potters should help potters. No gatekeeping. No secrets. Just a community of people who love getting their hands dirty. 🤎

Stop forgetting what you used. Start tracking what you make.

👉 **thepottersmudroom.com**

Built by a potter. For potters. Welcome to the Mud Room. 🏺

## TIPS FOR POSTING:

**For TikTok:**
- Use these as captions for a quick video showing the app or showing a "before/after" of trying to remember glazes
- Film yourself at the wheel or at the kiln and talk about the pain point
- Show the app on your phone — scroll through the features

**For Instagram:**
- Promo #1 works great as a carousel (one slide per price point)
- Promo #2 works as a long caption with a photo of your pottery + phone showing the app
- Use Stories to highlight the founding member deal with a swipe-up link

**For Facebook pottery groups:**
- Promo #2 is more appropriate (less "salesy," more story-driven)
- Lead with the problem, not the product
