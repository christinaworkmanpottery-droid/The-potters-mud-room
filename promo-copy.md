**POSTING TIPS FOR FORUMS:**

**Facebook pottery groups:**
- DON'T lead with "check out my app" — that gets deleted as spam
- DO lead with the problem ("does anyone else forget their glazes?") and then mention you built a solution
- Engage with every comment — answer questions, thank people
- Ask for feedback — people love giving opinions and it boosts engagement
- If the group has promo rules (like a specific day for self-promo), follow them

**Reddit (r/pottery, r/ceramics, r/hobbyists):**
- Use Post #4 as written — Reddit likes genuine, non-salesy posts
- Don't use emojis in the title on Reddit (it looks spammy there)
- Title idea: "I built a free pottery tracking app because I was tired of forgetting my glazes — looking for feedback"
- Be ready to answer questions in comments — Reddit respects engagement
- Don't post the same thing in multiple subreddits at the same time (they'll flag it)
- Wait a few days between subreddit posts

**Pottery forums / Ceramic Arts Network / Clay Buddies:**
- These are more niche — people will ask deeper questions about features
- Emphasize the glaze tracking and firing log features
- Mention that it's free to start — potters are cautious about spending money
- If they have a "tools & resources" section, post there

**General tips for all platforms:**
- Always include the website: thepottersmudroom.com
- Respond to every comment for the first 48 hours
- If someone asks a question you can answer with a screenshot of the app, do it
- Never be defensive — if someone says "I just use a notebook," say "Totally valid! This is for people who want a digital option too"
- Share your own pottery in the communities too — don't ONLY post about the app. Be a real member first.
